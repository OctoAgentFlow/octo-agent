# Daily X Queue User Interview Script

## Pre-Test Questions

1. What kind of X account do you operate?
2. Who is the audience for that account?
3. How often do you currently post?
4. Who writes or reviews posts today?
5. Where does source material come from?
6. What slows you down when turning source material into posts?
7. What makes a draft feel usable enough to copy?
8. What would make an AI-generated draft unsafe or unusable for your account?
9. What guardrails matter most for your brand?
10. What tools or workflows do you currently use for X content?

## Day-1 Questions

1. Was the purpose of Daily X Queue clear when you opened it?
2. Did you understand what context to enter?
3. Did you understand what source material to paste?
4. Did you feel blocked by not connecting X OAuth?
5. Did the workflow feel lightweight or heavy?
6. Did exactly 3 drafts feel like the right amount?
7. Which step was confusing?
8. Where did you hesitate?
9. Did you get to first value without needing help?

## Post-Queue Questions

1. Which draft was strongest?
2. Which draft was weakest?
3. Did the drafts use your source material?
4. Did the drafts sound specific to your product?
5. Did the drafts respect your guardrails?
6. Did anything feel too salesy, generic, risky, or off-tone?
7. Did you copy or approve at least one draft?
8. What did you edit?
9. What did you reject, and why?
10. Did the risk level and risk reasons help you review?
11. Was the review flow clear?
12. Did approve feel clearly separate from publish?
13. What would make tomorrow's queue better?

## Day-7 Retention Questions

1. How many days did you use Daily X Queue?
2. What made you come back or not come back?
3. Did it become easier after the first use?
4. Did the system appear to learn from your edits or rejections?
5. Did the queue improve over time?
6. Did it save time compared with your current workflow?
7. Did it create drafts you would actually use on X?
8. What was the biggest repeated frustration?
9. What was the clearest moment of value?
10. Would you keep using it next week?

## Would You Miss It?

Ask directly:

If Daily X Queue disappeared tomorrow, would you miss it?

Follow-ups:

- Why or why not?
- What part would you miss most?
- What would you replace it with?
- What would need to improve before you would miss it?

## Pricing And Pay-Intent Questions

1. Would you pay for Daily X Queue if it kept improving?
2. What would it need to do before you paid?
3. Would you pay for draft generation only, or only if review and learning improved?
4. How often would you need to use it for it to be worth paying for?
5. What monthly price would feel reasonable?
6. Would you prefer paying as a founder/operator, team, or agency?
7. Would you continue testing for another 2 weeks?
8. Would you introduce this to another founder or operator?
9. What feature request is strongest enough to affect willingness to pay?
10. What missing capability is not important yet?
